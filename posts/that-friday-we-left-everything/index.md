## It wasn't dramatic

People expect a big story. A breaking point, a confrontation, a moment of clarity in the rain. Ours wasn't like that.

It was a Friday. We were both tired — not the exhausted kind, the empty kind. The kind where you're doing everything right on paper but nothing feels like it matters. We looked at each other and one of us said, "What if we just... stopped?"

Not stopped working. Stopped working for other people's dreams.

## The first week

We didn't have a plan. We had a laptop, a shared Google Doc, and the kind of naive confidence that only comes from not knowing how hard it's going to be.

The first thing we did was pick a name. We wanted something that felt personal — not a made-up word, not an acronym, not something that sounds like every other agency. Since That Friday came from the most honest place we could find: the day it started.

## What we learned early

### Nobody cares about your logo on day one

We spent way too long on our brand identity before we had a single client. Looking back, the work itself was worth it — it became the foundation of everything we built — but we could've started reaching out weeks earlier.

### Working with your partner is not what you think

People romanticize it. "Oh, you work together? That must be so nice." Some days it is. Some days you argue about font weights at 11pm and wonder if this was a mistake. But here's the thing — there's nobody else we'd rather figure it out with.

### Small is a superpower

No overhead. No office rent. No team to manage. Just two people, fully responsible for every pixel and every promise. That means we're fast, we're honest, and we never pass your project to someone you haven't met.

## Where we are now

We're still small. That's intentional. We take on projects we believe in, we say no when it doesn't feel right, and we treat every client's brand the way we treat our own.

Since That Friday wasn't a business plan. It was a feeling. And every project we take on is a continuation of that same feeling — that work should mean something to the people doing it.
